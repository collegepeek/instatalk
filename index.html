The human voice is one of the simplest communication mediums;however,this medium is severely limited by language.
 
With the most spoken language, Mandarin Chinese, having approximately 1.05 billion speakers in a world of 7 billion people, a single language can, at best, allow you to communicate with approximately just 15% of the world population. We want to change that through technology.
Our Product
 
 
Fortunately, researchers at InstaTalk have developed a groundbreaking product that allows real-time, voice-to-voice language translation in the speaker’s own voice all from the convenience of their mobile device. InstaTalk is the result of a technological breakthrough that has been designed for the common individual and world leader alike. InstaTalk can travel with you anywhere, and you can keep all the world’s languages in your pocket.
 
User Experience
 
 
 
 
InstaTalk’s breakthough is impressive not only from the perspective of computer science but also from the perspective of user experience design. The end-user (Speaker A) simply puts the InstaTalk earbud in their ear, and holds out his or her mobile phone in the direction of the individual with whom they are having a conversation (Speaker B). The mobile phone of Speaker A receives the sound input of both Speaker A and Speaker B. When Speaker A speaks, the mobile phone translates his speech and plays it out loud to Speaker B in Speaker B’s language and in Speaker A’s voice. When Speaker B speaks, the mobile phone translates his speech and plays it for Speaker A in Speaker A’s earbud. By playing Speaker B’s speech through Speaker A’s earbud, sounds made by the mobile phone are not confused as they are only Speaker A’s words.
How does it work?
 
 
Instead of simply matching standard inputs with a library of outputs, InstaTalk uses deep neural networks to recognize a variety of speech accents, patterns, and speeds with increased accuracy. Deep neural networks (DNNs) work by a compiling a large number of independent inputs in order to make a single decision. By feeding the DNNs a variety examples and giving them feedback on whether or not their classification of a word was correct, the DNNs then get a better understanding of what combination of inputs yield the correct word and improve over time.
